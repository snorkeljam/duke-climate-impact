Nicholas School of the Environment

Climate Research & Environmental Leadership

Duke's environmental flagship leads interdisciplinary research across climate policy, marine ecosystems, and land systems. The 2025 State of the Climate Commitment report spotlights Nicholas faculty driving breakthroughs from coastal resilience to carbon accounting.

Flagship Partner Policy & Ecosystems 2025 Annual Report
Fuqua School of Business

ClimateCAP & MBA Sustainability Program

The ClimateCAP Initiative, led by Fuqua in partnership with 48 business schools, reached over 3,000 MBA students globally in 2024–2025. A joint 10-month Master in Business, Climate, and Sustainability with the Nicholas School bridges management and environmental science.

Business Education 48 Partner Schools Joint Degree
Pratt School of Engineering

DESIGN Climate & Clean-Energy Engineering

DESIGN Climate — a $750K initiative co-sponsored with the Nicholas School — deploys interdisciplinary student-faculty teams on real client climate challenges. Pratt faculty also collaborate on high-resolution remote sensing platforms to monitor ecological change.

$750K Initiative Remote Sensing Design Thinking
Department of Chemistry

Next-Generation Renewable Energy Research

Faculty including David Beratan, Jie Liu, Ivan Moreno-Hernandez, and Michael Therien are exploring novel materials and molecular systems for renewable energy conversion — from solar harvesting to advanced battery chemistries — under the Climate Commitment's energy pillar.

Solar Energy Materials Science Clean Tech
Duke Marine Lab

Oceans@Duke & Coastal Resilience

Oceans@Duke has catalyzed interdisciplinary ocean research and student engagement since 2020. The Marine Lab also leads Ready, Set, Resilience — a K-12 community science program partnering with NC public schools and resilience practitioners to build climate-ready communities.

Ocean Science K–12 Outreach Community Science
Nicholas Institute for Energy, Environment & Sustainability

Climate TRACE & Data-Driven Policy

The Nicholas Institute coordinates 12 interdisciplinary research teams advancing Climate Commitment priorities. It anchors Climate TRACE — a global initiative using satellite and AI-driven data to track greenhouse gas emissions at source level across every country.

Climate TRACE AI & Satellites Policy Bridge
Sanford School of Public Policy

Climate Policy, Health & Environmental Justice

Sanford is identified as a school with "distinct advantages" in climate policy and health justice. Faculty research spans energy transition governance, climate health disparities, and equitable distribution of mitigation burdens — directly informing local and national policy.

Public Policy Health Equity Governance
University-Wide Initiative

SCALES — Scholars for Climate & Advanced Leadership

A $10.45 million investment recruits outstanding early-career scholars across Duke's 10+ schools to advance climate breakthroughs through collaborative research partnerships. SCALES builds the next generation of climate scientists, engineers, policymakers, and advocates.

Office of Climate & Sustainability

Sustainable Campus Operations

Duke targets a 20% reduction in building energy use (pre-2009 stock) by 2030, alongside low-carbon construction standards for new facilities. The Office coordinates energy efficiency audits, renewable procurement, and the Climate & Sustainability Fluency Framework for all students.
